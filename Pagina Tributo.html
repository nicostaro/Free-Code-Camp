About Manu

  • Beginnings in Argentina

    • In 1995, Ginóbili moved from Bahía Blanca to the city of La Rioja to play for Club Andino. There he debuted in the National Basketball League, on September 29 of that same year, against Peñarol de Mar del Plata. That season he was named the best rookie in the National League. The following year, he returned to his hometown to play for Club Estudiantes.In 1997 he was selected MVP of the Mar del Plata Promises Game and in this same season he received the prize for the greatest progress of the LNB.

  • Europe

    • In 1997 he went to Italy to play two seasons at Basket Viola Reggio Calabria. During that period, in 1999, he was chosen in the second round of the NBA draft by the San Antonio Spurs with the 57th position. , decided to stay in Europe after an offer made by Kinder Bologna to play for two seasons. Here he managed to consecrate himself as one of the best players on the old continent, since in this time he won two Italian Cups with his team (2001 and 2002, being the MVP of the second), an Italian league (2001, being also the MVP of the same) and a Euroleague (2001), being the MVP of the finals.He was also selected to play the All-Star twice.

  • NBA

    • It wasn't until after the 2002 FIBA World Championship in Indianapolis that Ginobili joined the Spurs. In that tournament he played against future NBA stars Yao Ming, Dirk Nowitzki, Peja Stojakovic, and was the undisputed leader of Argentina who achieved a historic second place,24 in addition to being chosen in the best quintet of the championship. He suffered a sprained ankle in the semi-final against Germany but still tried to play, physically decimated, in the lost final against Yugoslavia. In 2002 he joined the San Antonio Spurs and played his first NBA game on October 29 against the Los Angeles Lakers, a game where he scored 7 points (3 of 9 field goals, including a triple of four attempts), grabbed two rebounds, he gave three assists, stole four balls, put up a block, fumbled once and committed three personal fouls. In his rookie season (rookie) he averaged 7.6 points, 2.3 rebounds, 2 assists and 1.4 steals, helping his team to become champions, winning his first ring in his first season. In his freshman year, Ginobili served as the team's backup shooting guard. He spent the first part of the season injured, and found it difficult to adjust to the NBA because of his style of play. Following his injury, Ginobili won the Western Conference rookie of the month award for March, and was named to the All-Rookie Second Team at the end of the season.
